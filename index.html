3D Rendering Engine

Using MATALB, this program implements geometry transformation and lighting calculations to render an image of a scene consisting of a single 3-D object. The point of this was not “modularity,” “reuse,” “extensibility,” or “good taste,” but instead a way to understand how a 3D rendering engine like Direct3D, OpenGL, and XNA handles the behind the scene stuff.

Variables:
• World-Space XYZ position and RGB value of color light source
• Variable RBG color of ambient light
• World-space XYZ position of the camera
• World-space XYZ position of point camera is looking at.
• World-space XYZ position of object and object orientation (represented as rotations around the x,y, and z axis).
• ‘Field of view’ and the “near” and “far” distances of the perspective projection viewing frustum.

Specs:

• Represents spatial coordinates as row vectors
• Performs respective world, view, and projection transformation to get into normalized coordinates.
• Flat lighting model calculates the normal of each triangle to perform diffuse and specular lighting calculations at the center of the triangle.
• Performs ‘Back-face Culling’ to remove all the faces facing away from the camera.
• ‘Z-clipping’ removes all facets outside the viewing frustum.
• ‘Z-sorting’ to remove artifacts that are blocked by other model facets that are in front of them.

Curve Smoothing

Problem:
- Low-resolution touch screen to high-resolution printed output leads to a very pixelated overlay of the users ‘doodles.’

Cool Facts:

1. Marketable feature (was on the box) in certain HP printers.
2. Living in San Diego assisted with the creative process.
3. This was my first time touching algorithms and image processing. I took a 3D Computer Graphics course the following semester because I enjoyed it.
4. My own handwriting (as apparent in the images) needs a smoothing algorithm.
5. Created when I was 19 years old, during my second college internship.

Tech Specs:
1. Uses a combination of Bézie curves, intelligent point averaging, and alpha blending to accomplish the transformation.
2. Only allocated by management ~1/7 the amount of memory needed to store a bitmap with the required resolution.
a. Overcame this by doing only calculating the part of the image the printer was currently printing.
3. Independent R&D feasibility study. Left HP with a finished solution.

MindLink

A wireless EEG headset monitors the user's brain activity to manipulate power, channel, and volume on a television. The team designed a custom software program, known as the EmoState Analysis Software, which interfaces and relays the determined command to a custom circuit. Custom electrical hardware manipulates the signals of a modified universal remote control in order to control the television and audio/video (A/V) receivers.

Wireless Electricity

A parallel LC tank-circuit (receiver) is powered by a AC voltage source at the resonant frequency of the receiver circuit. This creates a large changing magnetic field that induces a current in the receiver powering the light bulb (load).

Inertia Navigational Module

When your iPhone locates you within a range, that is an INM within your phone locating your position with inaccurate/noisy data and then recursively operating on a stream of noisy data to produce a statically optimal calculation of your actual location. After the initial point, it updates the data using a weighted average with more weight given to estimates with higher certainty.

Problem: Client needed hardware to give control systems data about vehicle position and orientation. Current solution was costly and third party.

Fun Facts:
• Learned a lot of cost-saving techniques for board bring-up.
• Beer was provided during the workday and I had recently turned 21.


Tech Specs:
• Utilized accelerometer, angular rate (gyroscopic) sensor, and magnetometer (compass) to locate orientation relative to gravity and the Earth’s magnetic field.
• Can be seeded with GPS to give absolute position.

Power Supply Validation

There is something inherently exciting about sparks and large amounts of electrical power but not to end-users. To ensure nothing bad happens with all this power, tests were conducted on large power supplies for a rack-mounted (5 RU) device.

Fun-Facts:
• The magnetic field from the large currents passing through the wires caused the wires to rock back and fourth.


Tech Specs:
• Python was used to control an electronic load.
• Used the pyserial library for communication to the electronic load.
• Dual-600W power-supplies in a power-sharing configuration were the DUT (Device Under Test).

Deep-Ocean Assessment and Reporting of Tsunamis

Ported the existing Tsunami Warning System residing on a buoy tethered to the deepest ocean floors to a sustainable (solar and wave powered), ocean going, robotic vehicle that can autonomously stay in one location.

Problem: Cables that tether a buoy to the bottom of the ocean are expensive to maintain. We wanted to replace buoys housing all the communication equipment at the surface of the ocean with WaveGliders—a marine robot solution.

BattleBot

Highly interested in grade school by the TV show Robot Wars and later BattleBots, I set out to build a ‘combat robot’ for my science fair project. Unfortunately, my dad restricted (it was his machine shop ☺) my ability to incorporate ‘weapons’ but I did come out with a pretty killer robot (pun intended).

Fun Facts:

• Constructed in Grade 9 of secondary school.
• All material was scrap (except tires)—9th grade didn’t allow me a large budget. ☹
• Motors were bought at a flea market and previously part of a computer to spin large magnetic tape wheels (a lonnnnnnggggg time ago).
• It can easily knock over my 200lb uncle (hits you low in the shins)—opps.
• I landed in the local newspaper (Asbury Park Press).
• Fashioned my own gearbox, but I do not know the gearing ratio. I do know it works.

Tech Specs:

• Remotely controlled by a Futaba PCM 1024 T6XH RC Airplane Radio Control System.
• Used Futaba servomotors to push micro-switches attached to a metal plate.
• Two switches per motor—one to move the wheels forward, one to move them backward.
• Turning was accomplished by turning one wheel forward and one wheel backward.
• Battery powered by two car batteries in series (24V) (also recycled)
• Everything was machined out of scrap aluminum.
